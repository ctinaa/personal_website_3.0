The Process

Research

Who is the audience?

  • Students curious about art pieces
  • Students who are there for a school field trip
  • Families on vacation or going for fun
  • Older people who have time to look at art museums

When would a person be looking for a prominent piece of art?

  • When they are at the museum trying to find more information
  • When a person is online looking up what pieces of art that are available
  • What pieces are available at the Louve

What does the audience want when searching for prominent art pieces?

I spent 15 minutes to create a list of features a person would want when searching for art and grouped my ideas together into clusters and took a look at what other websites did to help users find art pieces or general information about an exihibit.

User Interviews
I also created a small survey for people to fill out about how people find information about these art pieces. Although this is a small number of people, I wanted to get a small sample of people from Reddit (r/SampleSize), Facebook and Twitter. I posted this survey before I went to bed, and woke up to 105 responses.
If you would like to view the spreadsheet data, you can find this here.
Pain Points
Based on the data, I decided to look at the mobile version of the website and take a look at the pain points. I soon realized that there was no mobile version of the website to begin with. I wanted to focus on the first initial stages of finding an art piece. Thus, I went through the task of finding the Mona Lisa on the site.
Current Workflow
I spent 10 minutes going through the website to understand the workflow in order to get to a prominent art piece. I then decided to map out 2 ways to get to prominent art pieces.
Sketches

These are some of the sketches I thought of. I wanted to make it efficient for the user to search for a piece in general, because a prominent piece is subjective.

Homepage - Search
Redesign
I mainly focused on the first initial steps in order to get to a prominent art piece on a mobile phone since the majority of the people use a phone to view information about art pieces. Since not a lot of people also tend to bring their laptop to an art museum, I decided to redesign for mobile while keeping the same style the Louvre was displaying.
Key Takeaways and Learnings
  • Designing the mobile experience for an art museum is harder than I thought.
  • Keep things simple when making a menu, however keeping all the related items compressed was a challenge.
  • Allocating time to research and sketch before jumping into designing is important.
  • Make your designs accessible for users of all ages; espeically at art museums.
Next Steps for future iterations
  • If I had more time, I would redesign the desktop experience as well.
  • Add a mapping feature so people know where to go.
  • Add an art piece frame with shortcuts for users
  • Interview people who have actually gone to the Louvre.
  • With more time, I would prototype this on figma.
  • Find others to interact with this redesign with a particular task in mind.
  • Figure out if this is really what the user needs though more user research and interviews.
